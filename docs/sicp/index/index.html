Structure and Interpretation of Computer Programs

Structure and Interpretation of Computer Programs is the most useful (magic) book when you struggle on the computer science / programs / languages.

It teaches you to think the concepts in programming, rather just executing codes.

Programme progress is declarative, whilst implementation details is imperative.

Most contents are excerpts from the book Structure and Interpretation of Computer Programs. Follow the original book if you want detailed contents. Here is one full-text book in html hosted by MIT.
